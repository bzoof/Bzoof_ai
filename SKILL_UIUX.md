This skill helps a UI/UX designer or product team define visual style, interaction design, and branding alignment for an application.

## When to use
- defining the look, feel, and brand experience of an application
- creating UX direction, interaction patterns, and visual design guidance
- aligning branding, style, and product goals with implementation teams

## Workflow
1. Establish brand and style goals
   - capture brand values, tone, and visual identity requirements
   - define the desired user experience and emotional impact
   - document existing brand assets, style guides, and constraints
2. Define user experience and interaction principles
   - identify key user journeys, behaviors, and context of use
   - establish interaction patterns, navigation, and accessibility goals
   - specify visual hierarchy, feedback, and motion principles
3. Create design direction and UI system guidance
   - define color, typography, spacing, iconography, and layout principles
   - create component guidelines, responsive behavior, and design tokens
   - align with product structure, platform standards, and technical constraints
4. Validate and align with stakeholders
   - review concepts with product, engineering, and brand teams
   - gather feedback on usability, accessibility, and brand fidelity
   - refine direction to ensure clarity and implementation readiness
5. Prepare handoff and implementation readiness
   - document style guidelines, UX patterns, and component expectations
   - create a design checklist, acceptance criteria, and review plan
   - ensure the design supports the sprint plan and development handoff

## Completion criteria
- a clear brand and visual style direction
- defined UX principles, interaction patterns, and user journeys
- documented UI system guidance and accessibility considerations
- alignment with product goals, engineering, and stakeholders
- implementation-ready design handoff artifacts

## Questions to ask
- What brand values and visual tone should this application express?
- Who are the target users and what experience should they have?
- What accessibility, platform, or device requirements apply?
- What are the primary user journeys and interaction moments?
- What deliverables should be provided to engineering and stakeholders?
