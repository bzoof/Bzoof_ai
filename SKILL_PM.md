This skill helps a project manager or product owner organize kickoff, define sprint scope, align multidisciplinary teams, and capture action items for delivery.

## When to use
- setting up a new sprint with an architect, backend, frontend, UX/UI, DevOps, SecOps, and business analyst
- coordinating a project kickoff, goals, roles, and dependencies
- turning high-level requirements into a sprint-ready plan

## Workflow
1. Align on project purpose and sprint outcomes
   - clarify the problem statement, business goals, and target users
   - confirm the sprint goal, expected deliverables, and timeline
   - identify constraints such as compliance, security, performance, or operations
2. Capture team roles and responsibilities
   - list each role and its core contributions: architect, backend, frontend, UX/UI, DevOps, SecOps, business analyst
   - map dependencies between teams and handoffs needed for delivery
   - note any missing expertise or coordination gaps
3. Build the sprint plan
   - define scope, key stories, and acceptance criteria
   - prioritize work with clear owners and due dates
   - establish success metrics and definition of done
4. Facilitate kickoff and communication
   - prepare a shared agenda, decisions list, and roles matrix
   - surface risks, assumptions, and open questions
   - ensure the team agrees on collaboration cadence, tooling, and escalation paths
5. Record follow-up actions
   - capture decisions, next steps, and owners
   - create a concise summary for stakeholders
   - plan a review or retrospective after the sprint

## Completion criteria
- shared sprint vision, goals, and scope
- clearly assigned roles and dependencies
- prioritized backlog or plan aligned with team capabilities
- measurable outcomes and definition of done
- documented risks, assumptions, and next steps

## Questions to ask
- What is the primary outcome for this sprint?
- Which roles are available and what are their responsibilities?
- What are the critical architectural, security, or operational constraints?
- What customer or business validation does this sprint need?
- What deliverables should be reviewed at sprint end?
